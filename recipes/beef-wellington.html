Beef Wellington

A tricky dish to make right but one of the best you'll ever make!

When I first made this dish for my wife and parents all of our jaws dropped after our first bite.
All of us agreed it was the best thing any of us had ever tasted. Tender and juicy tenderloin
and a crispy pastry crust with a delectable layer of mushrooms and prosciutto in between.
I mean, I'm not even a mushroom guy but this was amazing. Definitely give it a try if you can
afford the tenderloin!

Ingredients

  • 1-2lbs Beef Tenderloin
  • Salt & pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 sheet Puff pastry (thawed or freshly made)
  • 8oz Crimini mushrooms
  • 1 Shallot, diced
  • 3 cloves Garlic, chopped
  • 1 tbsp Unsalted butter
  • 6 slices Prosciutto

Equipment

  • Sous Vide
  • Food Processor (optional)
  • Cast-Iron Pan
  • Plastic Wrap
  • Rolling Pin
  • Parchment Paper
  • Oven

Steps

  1. Set Sous Vide to 124°F.
  2. Season beef tenderloin with salt and pepper. Place in vacuum bag or resealable ziplock bag.
  3. Submerge and cook for 2 hours.
  4. Finely chop mushrooms, in food processor or by hand.
  5. Add shallots and garlic to hot pan. Cook until soft. Add chopped mushrooms and cook until all water has evaporated. Add 1 tablespoon of butter and cook until combined.
  6. Remove beef from bag and pat dry with paper towels.
  7. Heat oil in a cast-iron pan until shimmering. Sear all sides of beef until golden, 30 seconds a side.
  8. Spread dijon mustard all over the tenderloin.
  9. Lay plastic wrap on a surface and arrange prosciutto slices horizontally with each slice slightly overlapping each other.
  10. Spread the duxelles thinly over the prosciutto
  11. Place the tenderloin on top of the duxelles. Slowly roll the tenderloin with the plastic wrap and roll tightly. Place the wrapped tenderloin in the freezer for about 15-20 minutes.
  12. Roll out the thawed puff pastry with a rolling pin until smooth. Brush the puff pastry with egg wash.
  13. Unwrap the tenderloin from the plastic wrap. Place the tenderloin on the puff pastry and slowly wrap it. Cut off excess.
  14. Place the wellington on a cookie sheet lined with parchment paper and back at 475°F for about 10 minutes or until the pastry is golden brown.
  15. Slice and enjoy!
